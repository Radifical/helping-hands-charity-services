# Helping Hands Charity Services — Website Copy Document

**How to use this document**

1. Every piece of text on the site has a unique ID (example: `HOME-HERO-02`). Do not change the IDs.
2. Edit the text after each ID directly in this file. Leave anything you're happy with untouched.
3. If you want something removed, write `DELETE` after the ID. If you want something added, write `NEW:` and describe where it goes.
4. The `Guidance` notes show length limits where layout matters. Staying close to them keeps the design intact.
5. Send the edited file back and the changes will be applied 1:1.

### 4.3 Article 1

- `POST1-01` (category tag): Donor Guide
- `POST1-02` (title): How much of my car donation actually reaches the charity?
- `POST1-03` (excerpt, shown on cards): Many programs return as little as 25%. Here's how to read the fine print and make sure your vehicle does the most good.
- `POST1-04` (paragraph 1): When you donate a vehicle, the headline number, the sale price, is only part of the story. What matters is how much actually lands in your chosen cause's bank account after towing, processing, and program fees.
- `POST1-05` (paragraph 2): Some national programs return as little as 25% of a vehicle's value to the nonprofit. Others bury fees so deep that donors never learn the real figure. The difference between a 25% program and a 90% program can be the difference between a tank of gas and a month of services for the people a charity serves.
- `POST1-06` (paragraph 3): Three questions cut through the noise: What percentage of net proceeds reaches my chosen organization? Are there pickup or processing fees deducted before that split? And will I be told the vehicle's value before I commit?
- `POST1-07` (paragraph 4): At Helping Hands Charity Services, our vehicle network competes on price before we ever schedule a pickup. You see the strongest offer first, and the largest possible share goes straight to the cause. Transparency is the entire point.

### 4.4 Article 2

- `POST2-01` (category tag): Donor Guide
- `POST2-02` (title): Do I need a title to donate my car?
- `POST2-03` (excerpt): A missing title doesn't have to stop a good deed. Here's what we can do, state by state, to get your donation moving.
- `POST2-04` (paragraph 1): A title makes vehicle donation simpler, but a missing one is rarely a dealbreaker. Most states have a clear path to a replacement, and we help donors walk through it every week.
- `POST2-05` (paragraph 2): If you're the registered owner, your local DMV can usually issue a duplicate title in a matter of days. We'll point you to the exact form for your state and tell you what to expect.
- `POST2-06` (paragraph 3): In some cases (older vehicles, low values, or specific state rules), alternative documentation may be enough. The fastest way to know is to start the donation; our team will tell you precisely what's needed for your situation.
- `POST2-07` (paragraph 4): Don't let a piece of paper keep a vehicle from doing good. Reach out, and we'll handle the heavy lifting.

### 4.5 Article 3

- `POST3-01` (category tag): Impact
- `POST3-02` (title): Five causes your old car could fund
- `POST3-03` (excerpt): From ocean cleanups to NICU families, here's a look at the range of organizations your vehicle can support.
- `POST3-04` (paragraph 1): Vehicle donation funds an astonishing range of work. The same car can pay for beach cleanups, NICU care packages, surf therapy for veterans, or meals delivered to homebound seniors. It depends entirely on the cause you choose.
- `POST3-05` (paragraph 2): Climate and ocean groups like Protect Our Winters and Heal the Bay turn donations into advocacy and restoration. Family-focused organizations like Noah's Miracles support parents through the hardest weeks of their lives.
- `POST3-06` (paragraph 3): Animal welfare partners rescue, rehabilitate, and rehome. Health-focused causes fund research and patient support. Whatever moves you, there's almost certainly a partner organization that matches.
- `POST3-07` (paragraph 4): Browse the partner network, pick the cause that means the most to you, and let one unused vehicle do something remarkable.

### 4.6 Article 4

- `POST4-01` (category tag): Donor Guide
- `POST4-02` (title): Is a car donation tax-deductible?
- `POST4-03` (excerpt): The short answer is often yes, but the details depend on the sale price and your filing. Here's a plain-English overview.
- `POST4-04` (paragraph 1): When you donate a vehicle to a qualified 501(c)(3) organization, the contribution is generally tax-deductible. The amount you can claim usually depends on what the vehicle sells for.
- `POST4-05` (paragraph 2): After the sale, you'll receive documentation showing the gross proceeds. For most donations, that figure is the basis for your deduction. Vehicles that sell above a certain threshold come with additional IRS paperwork, which we provide.
- `POST4-06` (paragraph 3): Tax situations vary, so this isn't tax advice; a qualified tax professional can tell you exactly how a donation affects your return. What we can promise is clean, timely paperwork so you're never chasing a receipt at filing time.
- `POST4-07` (paragraph 4): Pick your cause, donate the vehicle, and we'll make sure the documentation is in your hands when you need it.
