# Helping Hands Charity Services — SEO Strategy

A keyword strategy built around the two highest-leverage assets on the site:
the **nonprofit landing pages** and the **blog**. Michael's input is the fuel;
the structure below is already built to take advantage of it.

---

## 1. The opportunity

Car-donation search splits into two intents, and we should win both:

- **High-intent / transactional** — someone ready to give: *"donate a car to
  [nonprofit]"*, *"[nonprofit] car donation"*, *"donate my car California"*.
  These convert. Our **nonprofit landing pages** are built to own them.
- **Informational** — someone researching: *"is a car donation tax
  deductible"*, *"donate a car without a title"*, *"how much of my car
  donation goes to charity"*. Our **blog + For Donors FAQ** capture these and
  funnel readers to the landing pages.

The single biggest structural advantage we already have: **every partner links
their own HHCS landing page from their website.** Those are authoritative,
topically-relevant backlinks pointing at our money pages. That is exactly what
ranks car-donation pages. The more partners, the stronger the whole domain.

## 3. Nonprofit landing pages = the money pages

Each of the 57 pages can rank for that nonprofit's branded donation terms,
where there is little competition and high intent. To make each one rank,
**Michael provides three things per nonprofit** (only the first is required):

1. **Mission statement** (already in for the current set) — keep it specific;
   it naturally contains cause + location keywords.
2. **Location(s) served** — e.g., "Los Angeles", "Ventura County",
   "nationwide". Lets the page target *"donate a car to [Nonprofit] in [city]"*.
3. **One "why donate to [Nonprofit]" sentence** — a plain, keyword-aware line
   like: *"Donate a car to [Nonprofit] and your vehicle's proceeds fund
   [what they do] across [area]."*

What's already optimized on every landing page (no action needed):

- **Meta description** naming the nonprofit

## 5. On-page checklist (apply to every new page/post)

- One clear **H1** with the primary keyword, used once.
- **Title tag** ≤ ~60 chars, keyword near the front.
- **Meta description** ~150 chars, specific, with a reason to click.
- **URL** short and readable (already the pattern).
- **First 100 words** state what the page is about, in plain language.
- **Image alt text** describes the image (helps image search + accessibility).
- **Internal links:** every blog post links to *Choose a Non-Profit* and at
  least one relevant landing page; landing pages link to *For Donors*.
- **No keyword stuffing** — write for a person first.
